Publication Impact Over Time

Our research output has been steadily increasing over the years, with a significant acceleration in recent years as our laboratory has grown and our research programs have matured.

We have published in top-tier journals including Nature, Science, Cell, PNAS, Nature Biotechnology, Nature Methods, and Science Advances, among others. Our work has been highlighted in scientific news outlets and has attracted significant attention from both academic and industry partners.

Our citation impact has been particularly strong in the areas of AI-driven protein design, synthetic gene circuits, and computational synthetic biology, where our work has helped establish new paradigms and methodologies.

Featured Publications

2023 Nature 542 citations

AI-Driven Design of Novel Therapeutic Proteins with Enhanced Binding Affinity

This groundbreaking study introduces a novel AI framework for designing therapeutic proteins with significantly enhanced binding affinity and specificity. The approach combines deep learning with molecular dynamics simulations to predict protein structures and optimize binding interactions.

Authors:

Chen K., Johnson S., Rodriguez E., Wilson J., Lin M., Hassan A., et al.

PDF Publisher Share
2022 Science 438 citations

Synthetic Gene Circuits for Programmable Cellular Behavior with Machine Learning Control

This paper presents a novel approach for designing and controlling synthetic gene circuits using machine learning techniques. The system allows for real-time optimization of circuit behavior and adaptation to changing environmental conditions.

Authors:

Wilson J., Lee S., Rodriguez E., Chen M., Tanaka Y., Johnson S., et al.

PDF Publisher Share
2022 Cell 396 citations

Microbiome Engineering with CRISPR-Cas Systems for Enhanced Bioremediation

This study demonstrates the use of CRISPR-Cas systems for precise engineering of microbial communities for environmental bioremediation applications. The approach enables targeted modification of specific microbial species within complex communities.

Authors:

Rodriguez E., Hassan A., Petrova A., Chen M., Wilson J., Johnson S., et al.

PDF Publisher Share
2021 Nature Biotechnology 317 citations

High-Throughput Screening of Engineered Enzymes Using AI-Enhanced Microfluidics

This paper introduces a novel high-throughput screening platform that combines microfluidic technology with artificial intelligence for rapid identification and optimization of engineered enzymes with desired catalytic properties.

Authors:

Lin M., Hassan A., Petrova A., Chen K., Wilson J., Johnson S., et al.
